## Terms in the formal model

### identifier

An index to a reference. An identifier for a source is typically derived from the name of a source. An identifier for a reference within a source is typically based on a numbering system that the source provides.

### applicability condition

A condition which evaluates to true or false. When false, the expectation is not applicable.

### expectation

The part of a reference that says what should, shall, or must occur.

### rationale

A why-explanation.

### reference (as used in this model)

A text that gives guidance on assessment of accessibility. It can be a whole document or any part of a document. References may contain references.

### relationship

How two items relate to each other.

### source

A reference, such as a standard or regulation, which has no parents.


## Terms not in the formal model

### clause

A reference in certain sources. For example, in EN 301 549, a numbered clause may be normative or informative, and may contain other numbered clauses.

### provision

A reference in certain sources, such as EU directives.

### reference (disambiguation)

'reference' may mean:

1. See "reference (as used in this model)".
2. A citation. For example, in EN 301 549, clause 2 ("References") lists the names of other documents.

### success criterion

A reference in certain sources. For example, in WCAG 2, numbered success criteria are normative and do not contain other success criteria.
